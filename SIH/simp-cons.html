Constitution Structure

The Constitution of India is the highest law in the country. It sets the rules for how the government works and explains the rights and responsibilities of citizens. Here’s a simple breakdown of its structure:

Preamble: Introduction that describes India as a free, fair, and equal nation.

Parts: Sections that explain how the country is run and what rights people have.

Schedules: Lists of things like state names and official languages.

Amendments: Changes that can be made to the Constitution.

Separation of Powers: Divides government power between lawmakers, enforcers, and judges.

Parts of the Constitution:
The Constitution is divided into 22 parts, and each part deals with different areas of governance:
· Part I: Union and its Territory (Articles 1 to 4) – Defines the boundaries and territories of India.
· Part II: Citizenship (Articles 5 to 11) – Rules about who can be a citizen of India.

Articles of the Constitution:
There are 395 Articles in the Constitution, and they cover everything from the organization of the government to the rights of citizens. Each Part of the Constitution contains various Articles that explain different rules and principles.

Schedules of the Constitution:
The 12 Schedules contain lists that deal with specific things. Here’s what each one includes:
· Schedule I: List of states and union territories.
· Schedule II: Salaries of top government officials like the President, Governors, Judges, etc.
