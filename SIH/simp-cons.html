Introduction

Constitution Structure

The Constitution of India is the highest law in the country. It sets the rules for how the government works and explains the rights and responsibilities of citizens. Here’s a simple breakdown of its structure:

Preamble: Introduction that describes India as a free, fair, and equal nation.
Parts: Sections that explain how the country is run and what rights people have.
Schedules: Lists of things like state names and official languages.
Amendments: Changes that can be made to the Constitution.
Separation of Powers: Divides government power between lawmakers, enforcers, and judges.

List of Parts, Articles and Schedules

Parts of the Constitution: The Constitution is divided into 22 parts, and each part deals with different areas of governance:
· Part I: Union and its Territory (Articles 1 to 4) – Defines the boundaries and territories of India.
· Part II: Citizenship (Articles 5 to 11) – Rules about who can be a citizen of India.
· Part III: Fundamental Rights (Articles 12 to 35) – Protects basic rights like freedom of speech, equality, and the right to life.
· Part IV: Directive Principles of State Policy (Articles 36 to 51) – Guidelines for the government to promote social and economic fairness.
· Part IV-A: Fundamental Duties (Article 51A) – Lists the duties of citizens, like respecting the Constitution.
· Part V: The Union (Articles 52 to 151) – Deals with the President, Parliament, and other central government structures.

Articles of the Constitution: There are 395 Articles in the Constitution, and they cover everything from the organization of the government to the rights of citizens. Each Part of the Constitution contains various Articles that explain different rules and principles.
For Example: Article 21 – Right to life and personal liberty (a very important Fundamental Right).

Schedules of the Constitution: The 12 Schedules contain lists that deal with specific things. Here’s what each one includes:
· Schedule I: List of states and union territories.
· Schedule II: Salaries of top government officials like the President, Governors, Judges, etc.
· Schedule III: Oaths and affirmations for government offices.
· Schedule IV: Allocation of seats in the Rajya Sabha (Council of States).
· Schedule V: Special rules for managing Scheduled Areas and Tribes.
· Schedule VI: Special rules for certain tribal areas in Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, and Mizoram.
